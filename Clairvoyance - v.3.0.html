What Is Clairvoyance?

// the core premise in plain terms

Regular Prediction Markets
Example Will Bitcoin reach $100,000?

Yes or No.

One event. One outcome. No structure.
Clairvoyance
Example Bitcoin reaches $100K
AND Ethereum processes more txns than BTC
AND gas fees drop below 10 gwei.

All three must happen. Express your actual thesis.

Real-world events don't happen in isolation. They influence each other, correlate, cascade. Yet every major prediction market today forces users to fragment their view of the world across disconnected, single-outcome markets — losing the very structure of the thesis in the process. Clairvoyance is built on one premise: betting on combinations should be the default, not an advanced feature.

What No Other Platform Offers

// structural innovations — not incremental features

Prediction Insurance
New · Unique

Buy coverage on a specific leg of your combo against oracle error, resolution ambiguity, or extreme edge cases — not against being wrong. If a market resolves incorrectly due to technical failure or genuine ambiguity, your insured leg pays out regardless of the stated result.

Insurance premiums are priced by the protocol based on historical oracle accuracy, complexity of resolution criteria, and current staked coverage pool depth. LPs who deposit into Insurance Pools earn premiums as passive income.

vs. Polymarket & KalshiNeither platform offers any mechanism for users to hedge against bad resolution. When UMA token holders resolve a Polymarket market incorrectly, affected traders have no recourse. Clairvoyance makes trust in resolution economically enforceable.
Thesis Versioning
New · Unique

When you update your combo's reasoning — adding a leg, removing one, changing your written thesis — the platform keeps a public, timestamped changelog. Your reasoning history is part of your public reputation profile. You can't retroactively claim you "always knew" something.

Top traders with well-documented thesis histories — including their publicly recorded wrong calls — earn higher Trust Scores than traders with identical accuracy but no documented reasoning. Intellectual honesty has economic value here.

vs. Polymarket & KalshiBoth platforms show you a position, not the thinking behind it. There is no way to distinguish a lucky guesser from a rigorous analyst with identical win rates. Thesis Versioning makes the difference visible and rewards it.
Adversarial Oracle Challenges
New · Unique

When a market resolves in a way a user believes is incorrect, they open a formal Challenge by staking CLRV tokens. The challenge routes to a randomly selected panel of users with a top-quartile Calibration Score in the relevant domain — not to the general token-holder base.

The panel reviews the resolution criteria, the real-world evidence, and the challenge argument. If the challenge succeeds, the challenger earns a fee from the losing oracle's slashed stake. If it fails, the challenger's stake is slashed and distributed to the panel.

vs. PolymarketPolymarket uses UMA token holders to resolve disputes — a governance token vote with no domain expertise requirement. A UMA whale with no knowledge of Brazilian politics can resolve a Brazilian election market. Clairvoyance routes disputes to people who actually know the subject.
Structured Settlement Tokens
New · Unique

When multiple legs have resolved correctly but the final leg is still pending — sometimes months away — convert your partially-resolved position into a Structured Settlement Token (SST). The SST carries a fixed yield backed by the locked-in winnings, accruing until final resolution unlocks the full payout.

SSTs are tradeable — you can exit the waiting period entirely by selling the SST on the secondary market at a discount to present value. Capital flexibility becomes a feature instead of a constraint.

vs. Polymarket & KalshiOn both platforms, capital is locked until final resolution with zero yield. If you've correctly called 4 out of 5 legs of a long-duration position, your money earns nothing while you wait. SSTs fix this.
Cross-Platform Arbitrage Scanner
New · Unique

Clairvoyance continuously monitors implied probabilities on Polymarket and Kalshi for markets that overlap with Clairvoyance combo legs. When the combo-implied probability diverges significantly from what a competing platform shows, the scanner surfaces it as a potential misprice.

This turns competitors into a permanent feature — their liquidity and market depth generate price discovery signals that Clairvoyance users actively exploit. The more accurate Polymarket and Kalshi are on simple markets, the more valuable their data is for Clairvoyance combo pricing.

vs. Polymarket & KalshiNeither platform acknowledges the other exists. Both are blind to cross-market mispricings by design. Clairvoyance's scanner treats the entire prediction market ecosystem as an information source.
Debate Markets
New · Unique

Two users with formally opposing theses enter a structured Debate Market. Each stakes capital. They make their argument publicly — text, data, charts. The crowd responds in real time by shifting the market odds between them. The debate runs for a fixed period; the final market price determines the winner.

Debate Markets have their own leaderboard. Top debaters earn a permanent Debate Record on their public profile and access to exclusive high-stakes debate pools. The crowd earns small fees for participating in the live probability-shifting during debates.

vs. Polymarket & KalshiNeither platform has any social or adversarial competitive layer. You bet in isolation. Debate Markets make prediction a spectator sport — and reward the crowd's judgment alongside the participants' performance.

Risks & Honest Limitations

// what could go wrong — and how we're thinking about it

Regulatory Uncertainty
Combinatorial crypto betting with governance tokens and NFT positions faces serious legal risk in the U.S. and EU. Kalshi's years-long regulatory fight was just for simple binary contracts. This is the hardest problem on the list.
Mitigation PathGeo-fencing for regulated markets. Legal counsel from day one. Potential Kalshi-style CFTC application for simplified tier. No promise this is solved.
Liquidity in Complex Markets
Getting meaningful two-sided liquidity into a 10-way combination market is genuinely unsolved at scale. Deep liquidity in simple markets does not automatically transfer. Shallow depth means punishing spreads and unfavorable combo odds.
Mitigation PathLP incentive structure heavily weighted toward complex markets. Bootstrapped liquidity in launch phase. AMM model designed to absorb imbalanced flow without breaking.
Oracle Manipulation Risk
Prediction Insurance and Adversarial Challenges create new attack surfaces. A well-funded actor could attempt coordinated oracle manipulation then claim insurance. Or flood the system with bad-faith challenges to drain slashing pools.
Mitigation PathStake-weighting on challenges. Insurance pool rate limits per market. Multi-oracle mesh makes single-point manipulation prohibitively expensive.
Smart Contract Risk
Complex combinatorial logic, NFT splitting, SST conversion, and insurance pool mechanics represent a substantially larger attack surface than a simple binary market contract. A bug in any layer could result in loss of user funds with no recourse.
Mitigation PathMultiple independent audits before mainnet. Formal verification of core settlement logic. Bug bounty program. Staged rollout with hard position limits until battle-tested.
Complexity Risk for Users
Combinatorial betting is genuinely harder to understand than binary markets. Users who construct positions they don't fully understand can lose money through confusion as much as through being wrong.
Mitigation PathShadow Portfolio mode for all new complex features. Forced scenario review before confirming any Tier 2+ position. Skill Passport behavioral gates slow-roll access to the most complex mechanics.
Competing Against Polymarket's Network Effects
Polymarket has hundreds of millions in volume, deep institutional liquidity, brand recognition, and years of network effects. Clairvoyance's Tier 1 must be as good or better — a claim that's easier to make than to execute.
Mitigation PathDon't compete on Polymarket's terms in Phase 1. Launch with features they explicitly don't have. Use their own data against them via the Cross-Platform Scanner. Attract traders who have outgrown Polymarket.

Why Combinatorial
Markets Matter

Real-world complexity: Events don't happen in isolation — they influence each other, correlate, cascade. Betting on combinations lets you express a nuanced, structured view of how the world works rather than fragmenting it across disconnected markets that lose the thesis entirely.

Better information aggregation: Combinatorial markets reveal not just what might happen, but how different events relate in the crowd's estimation. The correlation data alone is valuable to decision-makers, researchers, and institutions — not just bettors.

Market efficiency: Expressing a complete multi-leg thesis in one position concentrates liquidity and price discovery instead of scattering it. The market becomes more efficient when the full thesis trades as a unit rather than approximated by five separate positions.

Capital accountability: Structured Settlement Tokens, Prediction Insurance, and Adversarial Oracle Challenges aren't just features — they're a commitment that this platform takes capital seriously. Users aren't placing bets in a black box where bad resolutions have no recourse.
